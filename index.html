A Beginner's Guide to Valorant Agents

Introduction to Valorant

What is Valorant? Valorant is a 5v5 first person shooter game. Valorant is very similar to Counter Strike, but with one major difference. In the game there are 16 different characters, called agents. There are two sides, the attacking and defending. The goal of the attackers is to plant the Spike on the bomb sites and detonate it while the defenders try to prevent the plant or defuse the Spike. First to 13 round wins the game with an overtime if tied at 12. Each agent has 4 skills with one signiture ability that is free every round and one ultimate skill. From an orbital strike (summons a massive laser) to a lockdown, each of these different skills have their own different purposes. With each different purpose and a total of 17 unique guns, many different playstyles have arised.

Agents' Roles

Each agent has a role that define their playstyle. These are the roles of Valorant Agents:

  • Controllers are experts in slicing up dangerous territory to set their team up for success. By using their smokes and other abilities, controllers slice up and block the possible positions of enemies.
  • Duelists are self-sufficient fraggers who their team expects, through abilities and skills, to get high frags and seek out engagements first. Duelists are entry fraggers (killers). They clear out the spaces for their team and eliminate enemies with their skills.
  • Initiators challenge angles by setting up their team to enter contested ground and push defenders away. As suggested by their name, initiators initiate fight from afar with their flashes and try to get information early in the round.
  • Sentinels are defensive experts who can lock down areas and watch flanks, both on attacker and defender rounds. Sentinels can defend a whole site by themselves with traps or walls. In attacking side, they get info from flanking players or slow down a retake.
Brimstone

Brimstone is one of the original agents of Valorant. As a controller, he is responsible for bloacking enemies' sight and pathways with his abilities. Some of Brimstone's kit include smokes, molotovs, and literal lasers that let his teammates cross the paths toward the sites safely.

Weapon Types

There are many weapon types in Valorant. I squeezed them to four for easy understanding. They are the following:

  • Sidearms are very useful guns. It proves to be useful at starting rounds and economy rounds (saving money). Although inferior to the other big guns, sidearms are still deadly and is very dependent on skill. Some players even win rounds with just sidearms against fully-armed enemies.
  • Close Range weapons are mainly used in rounds with very little money. The spectre is almost always bought every round and the shotguns are mainly used for defense.
  • Rifles are the guns players are saving for every round. They are deadly because they can kill an enemy with just a headshot. The Vandal is the most used in the game and shows the skill (aim) of the player. The strongest and the gun with most cost is also here, which is the Operator. Even a body shot can kill if you have an Operator, and sometimes you don't know when you are going to get shot.
  • Machine Guns are not very useful in this game. This game revolves around precise shots while the machine guns make you run slower and kills slower than a vandal headshot. Still, it can be used on wallbangs and for new players because not many players have a good aim.
Assault Rifles

One of the most controversial choices in the game, Vandal or Phantom?

The Vandal and the Phantom are both one of the most used guns in the game. But many argue over which gun is better. Let's first look at what the differences are. The vandal deals more damage than the phantom, in long ranges. The thing with Phantom is when you get farther, the less damage you deal. The Vandal can one-shot an enemy over 50 meters. But, the Phantom has a faster firing rate and has more bullets than the Vandal. In conclusion, the Vandal requires a lot of skill to headshot opponents but the Phantom is much better at close range.
